Outdoor Workers & Extreme Heat

Impact on Street Vendors and Outdoor Workers

Street vendors and other outdoor workers face severe health risks during extreme heat events. Their prolonged exposure to high temperatures, combined with limited access to shade and cooling resources, creates dangerous working conditions that can lead to heat-related illnesses and even fatalities.

Key challenges include:

  • Prolonged Sun Exposure: Working outdoors for extended periods without adequate protection
  • Limited Access to Shade: Many vendors operate in areas with minimal tree cover or shelter
  • Dehydration Risk: Limited access to clean drinking water and bathroom facilities
  • Economic Pressure: Financial necessity forces continued work despite dangerous conditions
  • Lack of Regulations: Many outdoor workers lack formal workplace protections
  • Health Monitoring: Limited access to healthcare and health monitoring

Protection measures include mandatory rest breaks, access to cooling stations, protective equipment, and education about heat stress prevention.
