Essential Workers & Extreme Heat

Impact on Delivery Workers and Essential Services

Delivery workers and other essential service providers face unique challenges during extreme heat events. Their work is critical for maintaining supply chains and essential services, yet they often operate in vehicles or conditions that amplify heat exposure and health risks.

Key challenges include:

  • Vehicle Heat Exposure: Delivery vehicles can become dangerously hot, especially when parked in direct sunlight
  • Physical Demands: Heavy lifting and constant movement increase heat stress and dehydration risk
  • Time Pressure: Delivery schedules often don't account for heat-related safety breaks
  • Limited Cooling Access: Few opportunities to access air conditioning or cooling facilities
  • Supply Chain Stress: Increased demand for deliveries during heat events
  • Health Monitoring: Limited access to healthcare and heat stress monitoring

Protection strategies include heat-safe vehicle modifications, flexible scheduling, mandatory rest breaks, and access to cooling stations along delivery routes.
