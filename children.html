Impact on Children

About 559 million children are already exposed to high heatwave frequency and around 624 million children are exposed to one of three other high heat measures: duration, severity, or extreme temperature. By 2050, virtually every child on earth (over 2 billion) is predicted to face more frequent heatwaves, even if nations are on track to cap temperature increases to under 2ºC (as agreed in the 2015 Paris Agreement).

Children are particularly vulnerable to extreme heat due to their developing bodies and limited ability to regulate temperature. They have a higher surface area to body mass ratio, which means they absorb heat more quickly than adults. Additionally, children may not recognize the signs of heat stress or communicate their discomfort effectively.

Learn more about how extreme heat poses an extreme threat to children: https://ceh.unicef.org/spotlight-risk/extreme-heat

Key concerns include:

  • Dehydration and heat exhaustion
  • Increased risk during outdoor activities and sports
  • Impact on learning and cognitive function
  • Exacerbation of existing health conditions
