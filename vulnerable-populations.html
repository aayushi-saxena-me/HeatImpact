Vulnerable Populations & Extreme Heat

Impact on Elderly and Vulnerable Groups

Elderly populations and other vulnerable groups face disproportionate risks during extreme heat events. Their reduced ability to regulate body temperature, combined with often limited access to cooling resources, creates a perfect storm of health risks.

Key vulnerabilities include:

  • Physiological Factors: Reduced sweating capacity and diminished thirst response in elderly individuals
  • Chronic Conditions: Heat exacerbates heart disease, diabetes, and respiratory conditions
  • Medication Interactions: Many medications impair heat regulation and increase dehydration risk
  • Social Isolation: Limited social networks reduce access to help during emergencies
  • Economic Barriers: Inability to afford air conditioning or cooling costs
  • Mobility Issues: Difficulty accessing cooling centers or emergency services

Protection strategies include community check-in programs, accessible cooling centers, and targeted public health messaging for vulnerable populations.
